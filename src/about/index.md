# About the Linkage Governance Framework

The Linkage Governance Framework originated from a project led by the National Cancer Institute (NCI) and [funded by the Assistant Secretary for Planning and Evaluation (ASPE) through the Office of the Secretary Patient-Centered Outcomes Research Trust Fund (OS-PCORTF)](https://aspe.hhs.gov/creating-new-data-research-environment). This investment responded to the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services’ goal of strengthening the nation’s capacity to link data for research by addressing persistent governance challenges. 

Biomedical research currently relies on many disconnected data systems, and there is no shared governance approach for linking datasets across programs and institutions. The project set out to design a common linkage governance framework that would support creation and responsible use of linked, longitudinal, individual-level health data for research. The vision was that a common framework would streamline governance processes, reduce conflicts across policies and agreements, and unlock more complex, highvalue data linkages. 

To be most impactful, the framework needed to be both flexible and scalable. Flexible governance supports harmonization of diverse data types and data collected for research, clinical operations, and other purposes, while still honoring applicable requirements. Scalable governance enables linkage of numerous datasets from federal agencies, academic institutions, and other organizations. 

NCI engaged the Health Federally Funded Research and Development Center (Health FFRDC), led by [MITRE](https://www.mitre.org/), to develop and pilot this common governance framework to advance data linkage for patientcentered outcomes research and related health priorities. The resulting Linkage Governance Framework is intended to help researchers and data stewards decide when datasets can be linked and what rules and controls should apply to the resulting linked resources.

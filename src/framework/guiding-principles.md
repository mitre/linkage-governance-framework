# Guiding Principles

The Linkage Governance Framework endeavors to enable safe and secure data linkages that have been difficult to create in the past. 

These guiding principles articulate the core values that drive the framework’s purpose, processes, and objectives:

- **Principle 1. Ensuring access:** Investigators must have access to linked individual-level health data, as it is essential for advancing research that improves the lives of all Americans. 
- **Principle 2. Balancing linkage with protection of data:** Dataset Holders must balance advancing data linkages for research while safeguarding individual privacy, ensuring data security, and complying with applicable laws and policies.
- **Principle 3. Encouraging a patient-centered approach:** Governance decision making must incorporate patient perspectives whenever possible, and Dataset Holders’ patient representatives are important participants in the Common Governance Process.
- **Principle 4. Upholding authority and accountability:** The Linkage Governance Framework upholds the authority(-ies) of Dataset Holders, Investigators, and Requesting Institutions:<ul><li>**4a.** Dataset Holders are responsible for decisions about access, sharing, linkage, use, and dissemination of their data.</li><li>**4b.** Investigators and Requesting Institutions are responsible for conducting research and managing associated risk.</li></ul>
- **Principle 5. Advancing transparency and collaboration:** Effective governance relies on decision making that is transparent, collaborative, and cooperative to facilitate data linkage for research.
- **Principle 6. Ensuring documentation and reproducibility:** The Linkage Governance Framework requires the documentation of inputs, decisions, and outcomes to support the reproducibility and integrity of research findings and future efforts.
- **Principle 7. Promoting continuous improvement:** The Linkage Governance Framework evolves through continuous improvement to adapt to changing needs and enhance governance effectiveness.
